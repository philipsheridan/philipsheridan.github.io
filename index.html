Facilitator at the University of Leeds Institute for Medical Education (LIME).

Member of the esteemed Patient | Carer Community (LIME) that offers a valued patient and carer perspective to medical student education and practice.

In 2002 I survived a near fatal motorcycle accident that left me with complex injuries, a right leg below knee amputee and post traumatic stress. My first collection of poetry, Heart on the Mountain, published in 2012, offers a window into the importance the landscape played in my journey of recovery.

I grew up in Yorkshire where I live today. I hitchhiked around the world after leaving school at 18. When I returned to the UK I worked for over 20 years in the public sector as a therapist, team leader, practice educator, trainer and manager supporting children and families.

In December 2013 I had the honour and privilege to travel down to Westminster, London to receive the Award for Inspiration at the national Limbless Association Prosthetic and Orthotic Awards.
